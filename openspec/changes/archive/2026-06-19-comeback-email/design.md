## Context

The repo runs four daily background jobs as Vercel crons (`vercel.json`): `sync-matches` (`0 9 * * *`), `sync-news` (`0 7 * * *`), `prediction-reminders` (`0 12 * * *`), `quiz-reminders` (`0 13 * * *`). Each is an `app/api/cron/*/route.ts` GET handler with a uniform shape (`prediction-reminders/route.ts` is the cleanest model): require `Bearer ${CRON_SECRET}` (allow in non-prod when unset, 204 skip on missing-env in prod), set `maxDuration = 60`, run the dispatch inside `recordRun(kind, "cron", fn)` so exactly one `operation_runs` row is written, isolate failures (catch → log → zero summary, never a 500), and return the summary as JSON.

The email pipeline is equally uniform across `result-emails.ts`, `quiz-reminder-emails.ts`, `prediction-reminder-emails.ts`, and `welcome-email.ts`:

- A **pure renderer** (`prediction-reminder-template.ts`) builds email-safe HTML (table layout, inline styles, fixed hex palette mirroring the light theme, no `oklch`/`var()`/stylesheets) plus a plain-text part, with all copy passed in as a `*Strings` object — no DB/network, fully unit-testable.
- A **server-only dispatcher** gates on `env.resendApiKey`, **pages** profiles/predictions past the PostgREST ~1000-row cap (`SUPABASE_PAGE_LIMIT`, see `prediction-reminder-emails.ts`), resolves recipient emails from `auth.users` via the service-role admin client (`createAdminSupabaseClient`), builds localized strings via `getTranslations({ locale: DEFAULT_LOCALE, namespace })`, derives URLs from `env.siteUrl` + `localePath`, batch-sends via `resend.batch.send` (≤100/batch, all-or-nothing), and writes a dedupe/cooldown ledger row only for batches the provider accepted. It also reuses `email-sender-config.ts` `checkEmailSenderConfig` (warn-only) and the `withFromName`/`resolveEmail` helpers.

The comeback email is structurally close to the prediction reminder but with an *inverted* trigger. The prediction reminder targets engaged players who have a today-match they have not yet predicted; the comeback email targets *inactive* players — those with a past pick but no recent one — and asks them to come back. So:

- **Inactivity signal.** `predictions.submitted_at` (`timestamptz`, `supabase/migrations/20260513000000_init.sql:45`) is the per-pick activity timestamp. A player's `max(submitted_at)` is their last-active instant; inactive = that is older than the inactivity threshold. A player with zero predictions is excluded (they belong to onboarding/welcome flows, not win-back).
- **Actionability gate.** A nudge must lead somewhere. We only email a player when there is at least one confirmed, still-pickable upcoming match (`isConfirmedMatch && !isLocked`, `lib/match-utils.ts`) — the same actionable-match definition the prediction reminder uses for "today". When the tournament has no pickable matches left, no comeback email is sent (avoids a dead-end CTA, the same dead-end análisis.md §"Fricciones" #12 warns about).
- **Rank.** `v_leaderboard_overall` (already queried by `result-emails.ts` and `leaderboard/page.tsx`) provides `user_id, display_name, total_points, …, rank` with admins excluded. A still-inactive player who has scored rows appears here with a rank; a player with picks but no scored match yet (no finals) will be absent — rendered as "unranked".
- **Cooldown.** Unlike the daily prediction reminder (idempotent per UTC *day*), a comeback email must not fire every day a player stays inactive. The cooldown ledger holds a `sent_at` per recipient; a player is eligible only if their most recent `comeback_email_log.sent_at` is older than the cooldown window (default 14 days), so a churned player gets at most one nudge per cooldown period.

## Goals / Non-Goals

**Goals:**
- Send a comeback email to players who have a past pick, have not predicted in the last N days (default 5), and still have a pickable upcoming match — and to no one else.
- Suppress recently-active players (predicted within the threshold) and players already nudged within the cooldown window.
- Content that earns the re-open: days-since-last-pick, the recipient's current rank + points (or "unranked"), the next pickable matches, and a CTA to `/matches?picks=needed`.
- Reuse the existing cron + renderer + dispatcher + Resend + i18n + `recordRun` + one-click-unsubscribe patterns with no new infrastructure or third-party dependency.
- Honor `profiles.email_prefs` with a new `comeback` per-type opt-out (default opted-in), via the existing `isOptedIn` semantics, plus a footer one-click unsubscribe reusing `unsubscribe_token`.
- Be best-effort and isolated: a failure logs and records `status='error'`, never trips cron alerting into retry-storming, never throws past the route's catch.
- No-op when `RESEND_API_KEY` is unset; warn (not fail) when the prod sender is misconfigured, via `checkEmailSenderConfig`.

**Non-Goals:**
- Per-timezone send segmentation (M6) — the comeback cron sends once at a fixed UTC schedule, like every existing cron.
- The results digest (M7), the recap/comic digest (M9), push notifications, or any in-app re-engagement surface.
- Emailing players who have never predicted (welcome/onboarding territory, not win-back) or players with no pickable upcoming match (tournament-over dead end).
- Changing scoring, `v_leaderboard_overall`, or how standings/locks are computed.
- Tuning the exact thresholds beyond sensible defaults (inactivity = 5 days, cooldown = 14 days) — they live as named constants for later adjustment.

## Decisions

- **New cron route, fixed daily UTC schedule.** Add `app/api/cron/comeback-emails/route.ts` modeled byte-for-byte on `prediction-reminders/route.ts` (Bearer auth, `maxDuration = 60`, `recordRun` wrap, isolated catch, JSON summary, active-branding from-name override threaded through `getActiveBranding().emailFromName`). Register it in `vercel.json` with a fixed UTC schedule offset from the existing crons to spread load — e.g. `0 15 * * *` (after `sync-matches`/reminders/quiz). A single fixed UTC time is consistent with all existing crons; per-timezone is explicitly M6.
- **Inactivity threshold = 5 days, as a named constant.** Matches the análisis.md M11 copy ("5 days no pick"). Implemented as `INACTIVITY_DAYS = 5`; "last active" is `max(predictions.submitted_at)` per user, compared to `now - INACTIVITY_DAYS`. Days-since-last-pick is `floor((now - lastSubmittedAt) / 1 day)` and is surfaced in the copy.
- **Cooldown ledger keyed by recipient + time** (`comeback_email_log`): columns `user_id uuid not null references profiles(id) on delete cascade`, `sent_at timestamptz not null default now()`, with an index on `(user_id, sent_at)`. (Unlike `prediction_reminder_log`, the PK is not `(user_id, date)` because a player may be nudged across many cooldown cycles over the tournament; the natural query is "newest `sent_at` per user", so a surrogate or `(user_id, sent_at)` composite key with an index serves it.) RLS enabled with no policies (service-role only) — the exact posture of `result_email_log`/`prediction_reminder_log`. A row is written only after Resend accepts the batch, giving at-most-once-per-cooldown delivery across idempotent re-runs and crashes. **No historical backfill** is needed: an empty ledger correctly makes every currently-inactive player eligible for their first nudge (this feature *wants* to reach the existing churned cohort, unlike `result_email_log` which backfills to avoid blasting historical results).
- **Eligibility = inactive ∧ actionable ∧ opted-in ∧ off-cooldown.** Computed as a pure function over: profiles (id, display_name, unsubscribe_token, email_prefs), each user's `max(submitted_at)` (users with none are excluded), the set of confirmed-and-still-pickable upcoming matches, and the per-user newest `comeback_email_log.sent_at`. Pending = players whose last pick is older than `INACTIVITY_DAYS`, who are not opted out of `comeback`, who have no cooldown row newer than `COOLDOWN_DAYS`, and only when at least one pickable upcoming match exists. The pure computation is exported for unit testing, mirroring `computePendingPredictionReminders`.
- **Rank lookup from `v_leaderboard_overall`.** After the recipient set is fixed, load standings for those user ids in one query (as `result-emails.ts` does). A recipient absent from the view (picks but no scored finals yet) renders as "unranked" with their points omitted; a recipient present renders rank + total points.
- **Opt-out via `email_prefs` + footer one-click.** Add `comeback` to `EMAIL_PREF_KEYS`, `DEFAULT_EMAIL_PREFS` (true), `emailPrefsSchema`, and `normalizeEmailPrefs` in `lib/email-prefs.ts`; add the key to the `profiles.email_prefs` jsonb default in a migration. Drop opted-out recipients with `isOptedIn(prefs, "comeback") === false`. Add `app/api/comeback-emails/unsubscribe/route.ts` modeled on the prediction-reminder unsubscribe route: GET + RFC 8058 one-click POST, UUID-validated `token`, sets only the `comeback` key in `email_prefs` (no legacy boolean column for this new type), idempotent + non-enumerating. The email carries `List-Unsubscribe`/`List-Unsubscribe-Post` headers like the prediction reminder.
- **Renderer.** New `comeback-email-template.ts` mirrors `prediction-reminder-template.ts`: pure, dependency-free, HTML + text, fixed hex palette, all copy via a `ComebackEmailStrings` object, the rank/days-inactive block, the next-matches list, a single CTA deep-linking to `/matches?picks=needed`, and the unsubscribe footer link. HTML-escape all interpolated copy and data.
- **Localization & sender identity.** Resolve copy from a new `comebackEmail` namespace at `DEFAULT_LOCALE` (cron has no request locale, like every existing dispatcher). Use `env.emailFrom` with the active-branding from-name override threaded through exactly like `prediction-reminders/route.ts` (`withFromName`).
- **Run recording.** Add `comeback_emails` to `OperationKind`/`OPERATION_KINDS` (`record-run.ts`) and to `OPERATION_SCHEDULES` (`schedule.ts`). The dispatcher returns a `DispatchSummary`-shaped `{ emailed, failed, skipped }`, so `recordRun`'s existing failure-key classification works unchanged (`success` vs `partial`).
- **No-op / warn gating.** Identical to `dispatchPredictionReminders`: no-op + log when `env.resendApiKey` is unset; call `checkEmailSenderConfig` and warn (carry `senderMisconfigured` on the summary) when the prod sender is the sandbox default, without changing the resolved sender.

## Risks / Trade-offs

- **DB migration required (one new table + one `email_prefs` default change).** Both purely additive: `comeback_email_log` is service-role-only, RLS-enabled-no-policies, matching `prediction_reminder_log`/`result_email_log`. The `email_prefs` default change adds a key to the jsonb default; existing rows are unaffected because `isOptedIn` treats a missing key as opted-in, so no destructive backfill is needed. No Supabase Realtime is involved.
- **First-run reach (deliberately no backfill).** On the first deploy the cooldown ledger is empty, so every currently-inactive player with a pickable match is eligible at once — that is the intended win-back of the churned cohort. The send is bounded by Resend's batching and the existing pagination, and the cooldown then prevents re-nudging on subsequent days. If a large initial blast is a concern, the cooldown window/inactivity threshold can be tuned, or the first run gated — but for this pool's size a single nudge to the inactive cohort is acceptable.
- **Fixed UTC schedule misses time zones (shared limitation, M6).** Everyone gets the nudge at the same instant; players in the Americas may receive it overnight. Same trade-off every existing cron makes; per-timezone segmentation is the separate M6 bet and out of scope.
- **Threshold tuning.** Inactivity = 5 days and cooldown = 14 days are reasonable defaults but unvalidated; too aggressive risks fatigue, too lax risks losing the window. Kept as named constants so they are trivial to adjust without schema change.
- **Email fatigue.** A win-back email adds volume, but only to players who have *gone quiet* and only once per cooldown, and it is independently opt-out-able via the `comeback` key + footer one-click — lower fatigue risk than the daily emails.
- **`EMAIL_FROM` / sandbox sender (análisis.md QW1).** `lib/env.ts:46` falls back to the sandbox sender that Resend only delivers to the account owner. In this repo `EMAIL_FROM=no-reply@edselserrano.com` (verified domain) is already set in prod, so the feature delivers; `checkEmailSenderConfig` still surfaces any regression as a run warning.
- **`RESEND_API_KEY` unset (dev/staging).** The dispatch no-ops silently and writes no ledger for that run. Consistent with every existing dispatcher.
- **Locale mismatch.** Sending at `DEFAULT_LOCALE` may not match a user's chosen UI locale — consistent with all existing emails; locale propagation is out of scope.
